Semiconductor Fundamentals


From smartphones to satellites, semiconductors are everywhere. Tying together physics, chemistry, and electrical engineering, this easy-to-follow instroduction provides the background needed to understand devices such as transistors and solar cells.

  • the Fermi function
  • the density-of-states
  • intrinsic carrier density
  • dopping and carrier concentrations
  • carrier transport
  • generation-recombination
  • quasi-Fermi levels
  • the semiconductor equations
  • energy band diagrams

Understanding the process of drawing and interpreting energy band diagrams. Energy bands are a powerful , conceptual way to qualitatively understand the operation of semi conductor devices. They are the encapsulation of the device relevant specifics of the semiconductor physics. Drawing and interpreting an nergy band diagram is the first step in understanding the operation of a device.



Syllabus

  • Free carriers in semiconductors
  • Doping

  • The wave equation

Week 3: Equilibrium Carrier Concentration


  • The Fermi function
  • Fermi-Dirac integrals
  • Carrier concentration vs. Fermi level
  • Carrier concentration vs. doping density
  • Carrier concentration vs. temperature

Week 4: Carrier Transport, Generation and Recombination


  • The Landauer approach
  • Current from the nanoscale to the macroscale

Week 5: The Semiconductor Equations


  • Mathematical formulation
  • Energy band diagrams
  • Quasi-Fermi levels
  • Minority carrier diffusion equations


Course Learning Outcomes

  • Describe basic semiconductor material properties, such as crystal structure, bandgap, effective mass, etc.
  • Describe the behaviour of electrons and holes in semiconductors
  • Simplify the set of equations to specific problems
  • Draw and interpret energy band diagrams
