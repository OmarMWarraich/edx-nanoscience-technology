Semiconductor materials

  1. In a semiconductor crystal, each atom occupies a specific location in a "crystal lattice"
  2. Polycrystalline semiconductors consist of many crystalline "grains" with different orientations.
  3. In amorphous semiconductors, the atoms are more or less randomly distribute throughout the solid.

Graphene is a one-atom-thick planar carbon sheet with a honeycomb lattice

A unit cell can be used to reproduce the entire crystal simply by repetitively translating it. If the unit cell consists of only one atom, then we call it a primitive unit cell.

crystal lattice 2d lattice. the lattice points are separated a distance a in one direction and b in the other direction. Lattice plus basisSometimes a semiconductor material, there will also be a basis. At each lattice point, there will be additional atoms that are associated with it.

In 3D, there are 14 different ways,14 Bravais lattices, that we can arrange a set of points such that if we select any point and look around, the environment will be identical to what we would see from any other point
Three of these bravais lattices are very simple and r shown above. There's a simple cubic lattice, which has a lattice point at each of the eight corners of the cube, then there is face centred cubic lattice which in addition has an atom at each of the six faces and then there is a body centred cubic lattic in which there's a lattice point in the middle of the cube. Some materials crystallize in these 3 simple cubic lattices out of possible 14.
