Why is [h k l] plane normal to the vector [h k l]

Well the way we deduce the vector that is normal to a plane is to take the gradient. The gradient is the derivative w.r.t x in the x direction plus the derivative w.r.t y in the y direction plus the derivative w.r.t z in the z direction. If we perform that gradient on this function, we'll find that the normal is 1 over x intercept in the x direction and 1 over y intercept in the y direction, 1 over z intercept in the z direction.

Useful calculations on the crystal lattice



Building an electronic device on the surface of the electronic water, we might need to know how many atoms there are per square centimeter on the surface. lets say on the face of the cube there are four atoms - one on each corner, but they are shared with adjacent cube faces on the surface, so I can only count one quarter of each of those atoms for this particular cube face. Theres also atom right in the middle of the cube face, not shared with anything being the surface of the semiconductor so there's no cube above it. We conclude that the no. of atoms on this cube face is 4 x 1/4 for the ones on the corners plus the 1 in the middle. There are 2 atoms on this (1 0 0) face of the cube. The density of atoms per sq. cm. is 2 divided by the area of the face. Plug in numbers and we find 6.8 x 1014 atoms per sq. cm.
